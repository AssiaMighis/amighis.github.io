About me

My PhD, within the NumPEx – ExaSoft project, focuses on improving the energy efficiency of High-Performance Computing (HPC) systems while maintaining performance.

The objectives of the PhD are the following:

  • Monitoring of large-scale applications with limited data: Detect the behavior of the whole application by only using the monitoring data of a small number of servers; change the monitoring frequency depending on the needs.
  • Modeling and characterization of applications: Detect when an application switches from one phase to another; determine phase properties (I/O-bound, memory-bound, CPU-bound...). Software will be developed to detect and characterize phases of HPC applications during their execution.
  • Model the impact of various levers (DVFS, network and I/O reconfiguration, ... ) on performance and energy.
